    Who This Document is For

    This class documentation for the MoPub SDK provides comprehensive technical coverage of the MoPub SDK code and is intended primarily as a reference for developers.

    To learn about downloading and integrating the MoPub SDK we recommend reading the friendlier instructions on the GitHub wiki at: http://github.com/mopub/mopub-ios-sdk/wiki/Getting-Started

    An Overview of the MoPub SDK

    The MoPub SDK makes it easy to present ads to your users. We provide Banner ads and Interstitial ads.

    Presenting Banner Ads

    Relevant Documentation: MPAdView, MPAdViewDelegate

    You use instances of MPAdView to present users with Banner advertisements. You are required to pass MPAdView an MPAdViewDelegate which will receive notifications about ad-related events. The MPAdViewDelegate also provides the MPAdView with a view controller from which to present modals.

    Your MPAdView must be configured with an AdUnit. These can be generated at http://app.mopub.com. Once instantiated you instruct an MPAdView to fetch a banner ad by calling -loadAd. When an ad becomes available your delegate will be notified and you are responsible for placing the MPAdView in your hierarchy to make the ad visible to the user.

    MPAdView will automatically mediate any compatible third party ad networks that you have installed as needed. More information about integrating with third party ad networks is available on the wiki.

    MPAdView automatically refreshes ads and presents them to the user. The refresh rate can be configured via the web interface.

    Presenting Interstitial Ads

    Relevant Documentation: MPInterstitialAdController, MPInterstitialAdControllerDelegate

    You use instances of MPInterstitialAdController to present users with full-screen Interstitial advertisements. You are required to pass MPInterstitialAdController an MPInterstitialAdControllerDelegate which will receive notifications about ad-related events.

    Your MPInterstitialAdController must be configured with an AdUnit. These can be generated at http://app.mopub.com. Once instantiated you instruct an MPInterstitialAdController to fetch an interstitial ad by calling -loadAd. When an ad becomes available your delegate will be notified and you are responsible for presenting the MPInterstitialAdController by calling -showFromViewController.

    MPInterstitialAdController will automatically mediate any compatible third party ad networks that you have installed as needed. More information about integrating with third party ad networks is available on the wiki.

    Conversion Tracking

    Relevant Documentation: MPAdConversionTracker

    You may use the MPAdConverstionTracker singleton to notify the MoPub SDK that the user has opened your application. The MoPub SDK will ensure that the conversion tracker signal is only fired once per application install.

    In addition to conversion tracking, the MoPub SDK automatically performs session tracking every time your application is started or foregrounded. You may opt out of this by setting the SESSION_TRACKING_ENABLED constant in MPConstants.h to 0.

    Adding Support for Third Party Networks

    The MoPub SDK provides support for the following third party ad networks:

    • iAd
    • Chartboost
    • Google AdMob
    • Greystripe
    • InMobi
    • Millennial Media

    Support for third party ad networks is implemented via Custom Events (you can think of them as plug-in adapters that mediate between the third party network’s SDK and MoPub’s SDK). The Custom Events for the ad networks listed above can be found in the AdNetworkSupport folder. Depending on the installation method you have chosen your installation of the MoPub SDK may already include some of these custom events. Details on installing a custom event (for example, Chartboost), are available on the wiki.

    Building Your Own Custom Event

    Relevant Documentation: MPBannerCustomEvent, MPBannerCustomEventDelegate, MPInterstitialCustomEvent, MPInterstitialCustomEventDelegate

    You may use the Custom Event infrastructure to build your own third party integrations:

    You write a custom subclass of MPBannerCustomEvent to mediate banner ads, and a custom subclass of MPInterstitialCustomEvent to mediate interstitial ads.

    Once configured online the MoPub servers will instruct the MoPub SDK to build an instance of your subclass and instruct it to fetch an ad. At this point your subclass may call a third party ad network’s SDK to fetch an ad.

    When the ad arrives (or fails to arrive) your custom subclass is responsible for notifying its delegate (provided automatically by the MoPub SDK). In addition, you should translate any other events generated by the ad network SDK and call the delegate appropriately (for example, notifying the delegate of click events, or that a modal is about to appear). Special care should be taken to do this correctly and ensure that trustworthy click and impression tracking statistics are generated.

    MPBannerCustomEvent subclasses will receive an MPBannerCustomEventDelegate delegate and MPInterstitialCustomEvent subclasses will receive an MPInterstitialCustomEventDelegate. Please refer to the documentation on the wiki, and in this documentation archive, to learn more about the callback methods available on these delegates.
